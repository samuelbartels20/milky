Cookies policy

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on the hard drive or in the memory of the computer that access or visit the pages of certain websites, so that user preferences can be known by reconnect. Cookies stored on the user's hard drive cannot read the data contained therein, access personal information or read cookies created by other providers.

What type of cookies are there?

According to the entity that manages them:

Own cookies: are those generated by the page you are visiting.

Third-party cookies: are those generated by external services or providers such as Facebook, Twitter, Google, etc.

According to the period of time they remain active:

Session cookies: they are a type of cookies designed to collect and store data while the user accesses a Web page.

Persistent cookies: they are a type of cookies in which the data is still stored in the terminal and can be accessed and processed during a period defined by the person responsible for the cookie.

According to its purpose:

Technical cookies: they are the most basic and allow, among other things, to know when a human or a woman is browsing automated application, when an anonymous user and a registered user browse, basic tasks for the operation of any dynamic web.

Personalization cookies: are those that allow the user to access the service with some characteristics of general character predefined in your terminal or that the user defines. For example, language, type of browser through which you access the service, the selected content design, geolocation of the terminal and the regional configuration from where you access the service, etc.

Analysis cookies: they collect information about the type of browsing you are doing, the sections you use the most, products consulted, time zone of use, language, etc.

Advertising cookies: they show advertising based on your browsing, your country of origin, language, etc

Behavioral advertising cookies: are those that allow the effective management of the advertising spaces that are have been included in the web page or application from which the service is provided. These cookies store information about the user behavior through observation allowing later to develop a specific profile to display advertising based on it.

How to disable cookies?

The way in which the user can disable cookies will depend on the browser or browsers they use. All the browsers allow you to change the settings related to cookies. Here are the steps to take to reach this configuration in the main browsers used today, although the steps that are describe may vary depending on the browser version:

To access the cookie settings of the Firefox browser follow these steps (they may vary depending on the browser version):

  1. Go to Options or Preferences depending on your operating system.
  2. Click on Privacy.
  3. In History choose Use a custom configuration for history.
  4. Now you will see the option Accept cookies, you can activate or deactivate it according to your preferences.

- Internet Explorer: http://windows.microsoft.com/es-es/windows-vista/Block-or-allow-cookies

To access the cookie settings of the Internet Explorer browser, follow these steps (they may vary depending on browser version):

  1. Go to Tools, Internet Options.
  2. Click on Privacy.
  3. Move the slider to adjust the level of privacy you want.

- Chrome: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647?hl=es

To access the cookie settings of the Chrome browser follow these steps (they may vary depending on the version browser):

  1. Go to Settings or Preferences through the File menu or by clicking the customization icon that appears top right.
  2. You will see different sections, click the option Show advanced options.
  3. Go to Privacy, Content Settings.
  4. Select All cookies and site data.
  5. A list will appear with all the cookies ordered by domain. To make it easier for you to find the cookies of a certain domain enter partially or totally the address in the Search cookies field.
  6. After performing this filter, one or more lines with the cookies from the requested website will appear on the screen. Now only You have to select it and press the X to proceed with its removal.

What are the consequences of disabling cookies?

In the event that the user prevents the activation of cookies or deactivates if they have previously authorized them, may affect the operation of some functionalities of the website such as, among others, that it is not saved information on the acceptance of the initial cookie notice so it will be shown again on the next visit. This is the case of the cookies mentioned in the "operation and customization" section.

On the other hand, in the event that the user prevents the activation of cookies or deactivates them if they have been authorized previously, it will make it impossible for us to obtain information about user navigation through it, which will prevent or hinder the improvement of the information and content that we publish as well as its presentation. This is the case of the cookies mentioned in the "Google Analytics" section.
